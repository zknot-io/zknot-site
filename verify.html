How verification works
01 — PHYSICS
Hardware enforced
PowerVerify™ eliminated every digital data path at capture time. The evidence on this record could not have entered the chain any other way.
02 — MATH
Cryptographically signed
ZKKey™ produces a post-nonce signature after human review. The SHA-256 hash on this record is unforgeable without the signing key.
03 — YOU
Independently verifiable
This portal resolves any short code to its full chain entry. No ZKNOT account, no trust assertion required. The math does the verification.
What a verified record proves
  • ✓ This artifact exists in the chain — the short code resolves to an immutable append-only entry that cannot be altered retroactively.
  • ✓ The timestamp is device-attested — recorded at ZKKey™ signing time, not entered manually or after the fact.
  • ✓ The signing device is identified — the device ID is hardware-bound to a specific ZKKey™ unit, traceable to its registration record.
  • ✓ The SHA-256 hash is authentic — any modification to the underlying artifact produces a completely different hash, detectable immediately.
  • ✓ Chain position is sequential and gapless — the position number confirms no entries were inserted, removed, or reordered before this record.
Structural constraints — what this verification cannot assert
  • — Whether the operator correctly identified the subject of the evidence at time of capture. Human labeling errors are not detectable cryptographically.
  • — Whether the physical device used for capture was itself functioning correctly. PowerVerify™ continuity testing addresses this independently at capture time.
  • — The legal admissibility of the artifact in any specific jurisdiction. Verification is a technical attestation, not a legal certification.
