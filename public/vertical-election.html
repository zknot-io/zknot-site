The problem — why claims aren't enough
Post-election challenges don't accept "our systems are secure."

Every contested election produces the same document: a vendor attestation letter. A statement that systems were not compromised. That logs are intact. That images match originals. These are claims. They require trust in the vendor. ZKNOT™ replaces the claim with a proof any court, journalist, or citizen can independently run.

Problem
Ballot image provenance is unverifiable
Scanned ballot images exist as files on a server. A file can be overwritten, backdated, or substituted. The only proof of authenticity is the vendor's word.
ZKNOT™ Solution
Physical capture with chain attestation
PowerVerify™ eliminates the software data path at scan time. The resulting image is signed by ZKKey™, chain-recorded, and resolvable at verifyknot.io by anyone.
Problem
Audit logs can be altered by the systems they audit
Software logs are written by the same software being audited. An adversary with system access can modify both the evidence and the record of the evidence simultaneously.
ZKNOT™ Solution
Append-only offline chain, independently verifiable
ZK-LocalChain™ is hardware-enforced append-only. No entry can be removed or reordered. The chain itself is publicly auditable without connecting to any ZKNOT server.
Problem
Physical seals don't prove sequence
Tamper-evident seals record physical intrusion. They cannot prove the seal was applied before the evidence was altered — only that the box was opened.
ZKNOT™ Solution
TrustSeal™ cryptographically bound at application moment
TrustSeal™ is applied while ZKKey™ is signing. The seal ID appears in the chain entry. Any attempt to reseal after the fact produces a different chain position — detectable immediately.
Deployment workflow — polling place to canvass
How ZKNOT™ integrates with existing election operations

ZKNOT™ does not replace voting systems, scanners, or tabulation software. It wraps the evidence capture layer with physics-enforced attestation — the moment a physical ballot becomes a digital record.

01
Ballot extraction and staging
Poll workers remove sealed ballot boxes under chain-of-custody protocol. PowerVerify™ is staged at the scanning station. Physical continuity is confirmed before first scan.
Physics
02
Scan capture through PowerVerify™
Each ballot passes through the PowerVerify™ optical path. The digital data path to the scanner's onboard processor is physically eliminated. Images are captured directly to the ZKKey™ signing session.
Physics
03
Human review and ZKKey™ sign
The designated election official reviews the session summary on ZKKey™. Physical button press initiates post-nonce signing. The official's review is hardware-attested — not a software checkbox.
Human Gate
04
TrustSeal™ application and chain record
TrustSeal™ is applied to the ballot box simultaneously with signing. Seal ID is included in the chain entry. ZK-LocalChain™ records the entry with full SHA-256 hash and chain position.

05
Canvass and audit — public verification
Any auditor, observer, attorney, or citizen enters the short code at verifyknot.io. The chain entry resolves instantly. No ZKNOT account required. The math verifies itself.

Guarantees — what is provable and exactly how
01
This image was not modified after signing
SHA-256 hash of the image matches the chain entry. Any single-pixel change produces a different hash. Test: rehash the file yourself.
02
The timestamp cannot be backdated
Timestamp is set at ZKKey™ signing time, hardware-clocked, post-nonce. Backdating requires forging the signing key — computationally infeasible.
03
No entries were added or removed from the chain
Each entry contains the hash of the previous entry. Any gap or insertion breaks the hash chain at that position — detectable by anyone with the chain export.
04
The seal was applied before this entry was signed
TrustSeal™ ID appears in the signed payload. A seal applied after signing produces no chain entry. The only valid seal for this entry is the one in the record.
