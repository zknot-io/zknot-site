Mission
We build the systems that make evidence self-proving — independent of any vendor, administrator, or institution.

Chain of custody has been a software problem for decades. Software systems can be misconfigured, exploited, or simply lied about. When a forensic examiner testifies that a write-blocker was in place, they are testifying to a software state — which is ultimately a claim, not a proof.

ZKNOT™ changes the enforcement layer. PowerVerify™ makes write-blocking a physical fact testable with a multimeter. ZKKey™ makes unauthorized signing structurally impossible. ZK-LocalChain™ makes retroactive tampering mathematically detectable by anyone. TrustSeal™ binds physical and digital custody at a single atomic event.

The result is a chain of custody where the defense attorney, the judge, and the opposing expert can all verify independently — without trusting ZKNOT™, without calling our engineers, and without understanding cryptography. The short code resolves. The hash matches. The seal voided or it didn't. Physics enforces. Math proves. You verify.

What SDVOSB means for contracting
Federal agencies have set-aside contracting authority for SDVOSBs under FAR 19.14. The VA additionally operates the Veterans First Contracting Program, giving SDVOSBs priority consideration. ZKNOT™'s SDVOSB status makes it eligible for sole-source awards up to $4.5M (services) and $7.5M (manufacturing) without competitive bidding requirements.
