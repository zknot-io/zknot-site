How It Enforces

The post-nonce enforcement mechanism

A nonce is issued at session start and expires on a hardware timer. After expiry, the signing circuit is physically open. No key, no code, no administrator can close it.

Step 01
Session Opens
Operator initiates capture session. ZKKey™ issues a hardware nonce with a tamper-evident timer. The nonce is written to chain.
Step 02
Nonce Window
While nonce is valid, the signing button is enabled. Physical presence — an actual button press — is required for each artifact signed.
Step 03
Nonce Expires
Timer elapses. Signing circuit opens. No software, firmware update, or administrator command can authorize another signature for this session.

Use Cases

Where physical presence matters most

Any domain where the chain of custody must survive legal challenge, FOIA request, or hostile cross-examination.

Law Enforcement Forensics
Sign digital evidence artifacts at point of collection. The physical button press creates an unforgeable timestamped chain entry tied to the examining officer's presence.
Election Equipment Audit
Ballot tabulator audits require signed attestation of device state before and after handling. ZKKey™ ensures no post-audit signing is possible.
Investigative Journalism
Cryptographically timestamp sensitive documents at point of receipt. Prove the document existed, was unaltered, and was received at a specific moment.
Pharmaceutical Chain of Custody
Sign custody transfer events for controlled substances or clinical trial samples. Each handoff is physically attested and permanently recorded.
Corporate Investigations
Internal forensic examinations where the integrity of the chain must be provable to outside auditors, regulators, or opposing counsel.
DoD Field Operations
Offline-capable evidence attestation for forward-deployed operations. No network required. Chain syncs on reconnection with full integrity preserved.
Structural Constraints

What ZKKey™ cannot do

A system's trustworthiness is defined by its impossibilities, not its features. These are not policy settings. They are physical and mathematical facts.

By design, ZKKey™ cannot:
Sign any artifact after the session nonce has expired — the signing circuit is physically open
Export the private key from the ATECC608B secure element under any condition
Be commanded to sign remotely — physical button press is the only valid trigger
Produce a signature without writing a corresponding entry to ZK-LocalChain™
Be firmware-updated in a way that unlocks any of the above constraints
Produce a valid signature indistinguishable from one made on the legitimate device (each key is unique to the hardware)
