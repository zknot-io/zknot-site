TrustSeal™

Tamper-evident seal cryptographically bound to chain

TrustSeal™ is where physical custody and digital custody become one. At the moment the seal is applied, a chain entry is written — binding the seal's unique identifier, the ZKKey™ device, the operator, and the timestamp into an unforgeable record. The physical seal and the chain entry are inseparable.

The Binding Event

Physical and digital custody merge at one moment

The binding event is atomic — the seal and the chain entry are created simultaneously. There is no window between physical application and cryptographic record.

01
Seal scanned at application
The seal's unique QR or barcode is scanned by the ZKKey™-integrated scanner at the moment the seal is applied to the physical item. The seal ID is included in the chain entry payload.
02
Chain entry written simultaneously
ZKKey™ signs a chain entry containing the seal ID, operator device ID, GPS coordinates (optional), timestamp, and the hash of any associated digital artifact. The entry is written to ZK-LocalChain™.
03
Short code printed on seal
The seal itself carries the short code (e.g., ZK-4X9R-KM2) that resolves to the full chain entry at verifyknot.io. Any party can verify the seal's provenance by scanning or entering the code.
04
Tamper reveals VOID pattern, chain entry remains
If the seal is removed, the void pattern is permanently revealed on both the item surface and the seal backing. The chain entry remains — proving the seal existed, when it was applied, and who applied it. Tampering is provable either way.

Structural Constraints

What TrustSeal™ cannot do

By design, TrustSeal™ cannot:
Be removed without permanently revealing the VOID pattern on both the item surface and the seal backing
Be applied without simultaneously creating an immutable chain entry — the binding is atomic
Be reapplied after removal in a way that resets the void indicator
Be forged without access to the ZKKey™ device that signed the original entry
Produce a chain entry that doesn't include the operator device ID, timestamp, and seal unique identifier

Bind physical custody to digital chain.

TrustSeal™ is available in sample packs for evaluation and in volume for deployment. Custom sizes and materials available for specific environments.
